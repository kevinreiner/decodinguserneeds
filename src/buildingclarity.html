Problem Space

Navigating the Chaos: Identifying Key Challenges

The existing Knowledge Center lacked clarity and structure, making it difficult for users to find relevant information. The main challenges included: (1) Poor navigation and an unclear categorization system (2) Inconsistent content quality, (3) an overloaded FAQ section that did not effectively guide users, (4) insufficient resources to help advertisers proactively prevent fraud. Given these issues, a strategic redesign was necessary to create a more intuitive, accessible, and user-centric knowledge repository.

Process

The redesign followed the Double Diamond framework, ensuring a structured and user-focused approach

01 Discover

The initial phase involved an extensive content audit to identify all existing topics within the Knowledge Center. To benchmark industry standards, we analyzed competitors' knowledge centers and identified three potential structuring frameworks: (1) Topic-based (organized by key subjects), (2) Role-based (tailored to different user groups), (3) Task-based (focused on specific actions users perform). Additionally, we conducted user research to understand target audiences, their needs, and motivations for using the Knowledge Center. These insights provided a foundation for the subsequent restructuring efforts.

02 Define

After synthesizing our research findings, we identified the primary usability issues and determined the best structural approach. The decision was made to adopt a topic-based framework, aligning with users' expectations and ensuring a more intuitive and user-friendly experience. This approach enabled better discoverability of key resources and streamlined navigation.

03 Develop

To build a robust information architecture, we conducted a card sorting experiment, allowing us to gain insights into how users naturally categorize information. The results guided the creation of a new sitemap, designed with the following principles: Flexibility: The structure accommodates future topics and content expansion. Consistency: Uniform categorization enhances user comprehension. User-centricity: Navigation aligns with users' mental models, improving accessibility.

04 Deliver

In the final phase, all content pages were developed and organized within a dedicated space in Confluence, following the new sitemap. Company-wide feedback was collected and incorporated, ensuring that the structure met both user needs and business goals. The redesigned Knowledge Center was then successfully deployed via Scroll Viewport, providing a seamless and efficient user experience.
